BACKGROUND

Cloud computing has received significant attention recently, and it often consists of SaaS (Software as a Service), PaaS (Platform as a Service), and IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service). SaaS represents a new way of software delivery and development, and it involves many techniques from a variety of topics such as software architecture, database, code generation, control theory, monitoring, testing, simulation, operating systems, fault-tolerant computing, scheduling, data mining, P2P protocols, QoS, pricing, evolution, and caching.

CALL FOR PAPERS

This workshop calls for both theoretical work related to SaaS including modeling, analysis, simulation as well as system design and development. Also, this workshop encourages survey papers on various aspects of SaaS. The following topics are included (but not limited to) in this special issue:

  • SaaS architecture, multi-tenancy design, design patterns, tenant composition
  • Datastore design, data schema and mapping, dynamic indexing, database partitioning for SaaS
  • SaaS Runtime infrastructure, monitoring, data provenance, control, elastic computing, provisioning
  • Fault-tolerant computing, automated recovery, P2P protocols for SaaS
  • Scheduling, service duplication, fairness, load balance for SaaS
  • Ontology, reasoning, data mining, evolution for SaaS
  • Continuous testing and validation, modeling and simulation for SaaS
  • Tenant application development including customization, verification, simulation, and testing on SaaS
  • SaaS QoS, security, reliability, availability, service bus mechanisms
  • SaaS and PaaS interaction including caching, multi-tenancy architecture and caching
  • Metadata, modeling and simulation of SaaS
  • Security, reliability, trustworthiness of SaaS systems
  • Software engineering of SaaS systems including requirement engineering for tenant applications, design architecture of multi-tenancy architecture
  • Testing, verification, and model checking of SaaS
  • Big data for SaaS analysis
  • SaaS applications (eScience, Smart Grid, Internet of Things, finance, e-business), case studies, systems
